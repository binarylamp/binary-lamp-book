# Commoditize

## Why this word matters

Imagine a tool that once felt special.

Only a few companies could build it. Only trained people could use it well. Customers paid more because the tool was rare, difficult, or clearly better than other choices.

Then the market changes. More companies can make similar tools. Prices fall. Customers stop caring so much about which brand made the product. They begin to ask a simpler question: which one is cheaper, faster, or easier to get?

In business English, people say the product has been commoditized.

This word matters in IT because technology often moves from special to ordinary. Cloud storage, video calls, website hosting, and even some AI features can become easier to copy and harder to sell as unique.

When people say AI may commoditize software, they mean software may become less rare. If many people can create similar apps or code with AI tools, the value may move away from the software itself and toward trust, distribution, data, infrastructure, design, or real-world operations.

## Word Origin

Commoditize comes from commodity.

Commodity is connected to Latin commodus, meaning convenient, suitable, or useful.

Over time, commodity came to mean a useful thing that can be bought and sold. In modern business, it often means a basic product that is not very different from other products of the same type.

The ending -ize means to make something become a certain thing.

So commoditize means to make something become like a commodity.

## Original Meaning

A commodity is a product that buyers see as mostly interchangeable.

For example, one bag of ordinary sugar is not very different from another bag of ordinary sugar. One liter of gasoline may be treated much like another liter of gasoline.

The seller matters less. The price matters more.

To commoditize something means to make it lose some of its special difference in the eyes of buyers.

This does not always mean the product is bad. It means the product has become common enough that customers compare it more by price, availability, and convenience.

## Meaning in IT

In IT, commoditize means to make a technology, feature, skill, or service less unique because many people or companies can offer something similar.

A new technology often begins as rare and expensive. Later, tools improve. Knowledge spreads. Competitors copy the idea. Standards appear. The technology becomes easier to buy, use, or replace.

That process can commoditize the technology.

For example, basic cloud storage has become close to a commodity for many users. Many services can store files online. The real difference may be price, security, integration, or trust.

AI can also commoditize parts of digital work. If AI tools help many people write code, create images, summarize documents, or build simple apps, those outputs may become more abundant.

When something becomes abundant, it can become harder to charge a high price for it.

This is why commoditize is not only a technical word. It is also a strategy word. It asks:

- What is still rare?
- What is easy to copy?
- What do users truly value?
- Where does trust come from?
- What part of the system is difficult to replace?

## Examples

- AI tools may commoditize simple software features.
- Cloud providers helped commoditize basic computing power.
- If every app has the same chatbot, the chatbot feature may become commoditized.
- The company wants to avoid being commoditized by offering better support and stronger security.
- Open standards can commoditize a market by making it easier to switch providers.

You may also see related forms:

- The market is becoming commoditized.
- Software commoditization is changing how investors think about value.
- A commoditized feature is difficult to sell as a premium feature.

## Japanese Notes

Commoditize は、日本語では「コモディティ化する」と訳されることが多いです。

コモディティ化とは、商品やサービスの違いが見えにくくなり、価格や入手しやすさで比較されやすくなることです。

IT では、「その技術が特別ではなくなる」という意味で使われます。

たとえば、以前は高度だった機能が、多くのツールで簡単に使えるようになると、その機能は commoditized したと言えます。

注意したいのは、commoditize は「価値がゼロになる」という意味ではないことです。価値の場所が変わる、という理解が大切です。

## Related Words

- Commodity: a basic product that buyers see as interchangeable
- Differentiate: to make something clearly different from others
- Moat: a business advantage that is hard for competitors to copy
- Scarcity: the state of being limited or rare
- Infrastructure: the basic systems that support a service
- Automation: using machines or software to perform work
- Standardization: making things follow common rules or formats
